How it works

Doyen Consulting Group carefully listens to the problem or crisis your family is experiencing

We then integrate a three-pronged approach to change:

Create connection

Evidence proves that social support and meaningful relationships are just as crucial to wellbeing as food and water. Unfortunately, addiction and mental illness often to lead to withdrawal, isolation and abandonment from those closest to them. Our goal is to build authentic connection outside the current support system. We begin this step by providing families with a specific script to introduce this Doyen Advisor.

Build a Relationship of trust

We involve your loved one in the process whenever possible, as early in the process as possible. We believe that their point of view is crucial to their successful recovery, and we want to communicate that to them. Building a relationship of rapport and trust allows us to come alongside them as we all, collectively, navigate their recovery journey.

Frequently Asked Questions

My loved one is refusing help. How can we get them involved?
While we cannot FORCE anyone to change, we can create motivation to change. Though our process is inclusive and invitational, there are still times when a loved one refuses to engage. When this occurs, we carefully coach the family system to identify limits and boundaries that they can set to increase motivation for engagement.
